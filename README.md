### Customer Demographics Analysis 

## 1. Proportion of male and female customers
The customer base is almost evenly split between male and female customers, with no major dominance by either gender.

## 2. Senior citizens churn comparison
Senior citizens show a higher churn rate compared to non-senior customers, indicating they are more likely to leave the service.

## 3. Customer segmentation by tenure

## 4. Churn rate by tenure segment

Customers with shorter tenure generally have higher monthly charges, while long-tenure customers tend to pay slightly less.

## 6. Monthly charges vs tenure by gender
Both genders show a similar pattern: higher monthly charges and lower tenure are associated with higher churn.

### Contract & Billing Insights

## 1. Churn rate by contract type
Month-to-month contracts show the highest churn, while one-year and two-year contracts have much lower churn.

## 2. Payment method and churn distribution
Electronic check users show higher churn compared to customers using credit cards or bank transfers.

## 3. Average total charges by contract
Customers with long-term contracts have higher total charges due to longer tenure.

## 4. Monthly charges by payment method
Monthly charges vary across payment methods, with electronic check users often paying higher amounts.

## 5. Paperless billing and churn
Customers using paperless billing have a slightly higher churn rate.

## 6. Tenure by contract type
Long-term contracts are strongly associated with longer customer tenure.

## 7. Billing KPIs with filters
Billing KPIs dynamically update using contract type and payment method filters.

### Churn Prediction & Key Drivers 

## 1. Overall churn rate
The overall churn rate indicates a significant portion of customers leave the service.

## 2. Strongest churn drivers
Contract type, tenure, monthly charges, and internet service type are the strongest factors influencing churn.

## 3. High-risk customer segments
Low-tenure, month-to-month, fiber optic customers with high monthly charges are most likely to churn.

## 4. Revenue impact
Lost customers contribute to revenue loss, while retained long-term customers generate higher lifetime value.

## 5. Churn risk score
Customers with higher churn risk scores are concentrated in short-tenure and high-charge segments.

## 6. Churn concentration
Churn is most concentrated among customers with multiple risk factors combined.
